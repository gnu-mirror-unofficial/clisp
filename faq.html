CLISP FAQ list

This is a list of frequently asked questions about CLISP on the CLISP mailing lists and the USENET newsgroup comp.lang.lisp. All the legitimate technical question are addressed in the CLISP documentation (implementation notes, man page, etc), and for such questions this list provides a link into the docs. The frequently asked political questions are answered here in full detail (meaning that no further explanations of the issues will be provided).

Please submit more questions (and answers!)

Why is CLISP using menorah as the logo?

If you must have some answer and you do not care whether it is correct or not, you may simply think that Common Lisp brings the Light to a programmer, and CLISP is a vehicle that carries the Light.

History

CLISP has been using the menorah for the logo since the project was first started in the late 1980-ies by Bruno Haible and Michael Stoll. This probably reflects the authors' affection toward the Jewish people, Judaism or the State of Israel (neither of the two original authors is Jewish by birth). You may ask the original authors for details yourself. Both of them are very busy though, so do not expect a prompt reply.

Shouldn't the logo be changed now due to the current political developments in the Middle East?

The CLISP developers, both the original creators and the current maintainers, do not subscribe to the mainstream view that blames the Jews for everything from high oil prices and Islamic extremism to El Niño and Sun spots.

Aren't there other political issues of concern?

Yes, there are! For example, in 1989 the communist government of the People's Republic of China murdered some 3000+ student human rights protesters at the Tienanmen square in Beijing, and people appear to have already forgotten this crime. A note to that effect was kept in the file timezone.lisp until 2002, when we decided that is was not visible enough there and moved it here.

How do I ask for help?

If you have a question about CLISP, you have the following options (listed in the order of decreasing audience size):

USENET group comp.lang.lisp
This is the right place to ask all general Lisp questions, such as "how do I find a character in a string?"
CLISP User Mailing List <clisp-list>
This is the right place to ask user-level CLISP-specific questions, such as "how do I save my CLISP image?"
CLISP Developer Mailing List <clisp-devel>
This is the right place to discuss CLISP internals, submit patches, offer help. You must be subscribed to post.
Individual CLISP developers
This is never the right thing to do, unless you want to hire this developer to do CLISP consulting (commercial support, custom enhancements etc). This is actually for your own benefit: the CLISP developers are so busy, they might get weeks to answer your question; as well as for the benefit of the other CLISP users, since the mailing lists are publicly archived.

where is the IDE?

Use GNU Emacs, running CLISP as an "inferior lisp" (i.e., CLISP running as an Emacs sub-process). Some people prefer ILISP to the standard Emacs facilities.

See also The Common Lisp Cookbook

invalid byte #x94 in CHARSET:ASCII conversion

This means that you are trying to read a non-ASCII character from a stream which has ASCII STREAM-EXTERNAL-FORMAT. The default is described in The Fine Manual.

Display all 1259 possibilities? (y or n)

CLISP uses GNU readline for command line editing and completion. You get this "Display all 1259 possibilities" message (and sometimes many screens of symbols) when you hit TAB too many times. You can turn this feature off if you are using Emacs.
